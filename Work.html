Work Experience

Working closely with seasoned IT experts who can serve as mentors is a common aspect of OJT. They can act as a guide and offer insightful information about industry best practices, cutting-edge technologies, and practical applications. You can learn useful skills from these mentors, get constructive criticism on your work, and obtain a broader comprehension of the IT sector.

Scenario: A critical server or hardware component fails, leading to service disruption. Approach: Notify the appropriate teams about the hardware failure and start the process of troubleshooting or replacing the faulty component. Follow established procedures for hardware maintenance, coordinate with vendors or technical support teams, and restore services as quickly as possible to minimize downtime.

Working alongside fellow OJTs provides an opportunity for collaboration, knowledge sharing, and mutual support. By interacting with co-OJTs, you can learn from their experiences, exchange ideas, and gain insights into different approaches to IT challenges. Collaborating on projects or problem-solving tasks with your peers can enhance your teamwork, communication, and interpersonal skills. It also allows you to build a network of connections within the IT community that can be valuable for future collaborations or career opportunities.

Computer Maintenance and Troubleshooting Project


Demonstrated proficiency in computer maintenance and troubleshooting by resolving various hardware and software issues for clients. Provided timely and effective technical support to ensure optimal performance and functionality of computer systems.


Most Important Things in the IT Profession I've Learned During OJT


The information technology industry is continually changing, with new approaches, frameworks, and technologies appearing frequently. It is crucial to have an attitude of constant learning and keep up with the most recent developments. This includes doing independent research, learning new tools and languages, going to workshops or conferences, and looking for professional development opportunities. IT workers that embrace lifelong learning are better able to adjust to shifting technological environments and maintain their position as leaders in the field.

Become a Good and Competent IT Professional


To become a competent IT professional, it's important to have a mindset of continuous improvement. This involves staying updated with the latest trends, technologies, and best practices in the IT industry. It also means actively seeking opportunities for professional development, attending workshops or conferences, participating in online communities, and engaging in self-study to enhance your skills and expand your knowledge.

Bring Impact and Good Influence to the Next Batch of OJTs


Offer yourself as a mentor to the next batch of OJTs. Share your experiences, insights, and lessons learned during your own OJT. Provide guidance, support, and advice to help them navigate through their own OJT journey. Offer assistance in understanding technical concepts, resolving challenges, and setting career goals. Your mentorship can significantly contribute to their growth and development.
